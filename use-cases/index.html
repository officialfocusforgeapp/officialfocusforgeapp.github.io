Use Cases

One product, four very different operating modes.

FocusForge is not only for one type of user. The same blocking engine can be configured for solo discipline, partner accountability, family controls, or recovery-oriented use.

Solo

People who know exactly what needs to be cut off

Block the apps and websites that keep taking the day, set saved schedules, and use Lock Ins when the work has to happen now.

  • Fast setup for recurring problem apps
  • Schedule-based enforcement
  • Theme and block-screen customization
Partner

Shared accountability without constant supervision

Use a partner seat for session approvals, release states, alerts, and intervention that still feels structured.

  • Partner approval states
  • Shared sessions and alerts
  • Deep-link invites and public profile sharing
Family

Household-level control for linked devices

Parents can manage up to three linked child devices, apply scheduling rules, and keep household controls in one place.

  • Linked child-device management
  • Household scheduling
  • Parent-focused rule management
Recovery

When gentle reminders are not enough

For users who need a system that feels more corrective and more protective, FocusForge can lean into stricter interventions and recovery tools.

  • Addict Mode / Risk Group flows
  • Harder exits and unlock gauntlets
  • Recovery Tools and tone adjustment
What changes between them

The engine stays the same. The enforcement logic changes.

That is what keeps the site understandable. We are not pretending these are separate products.

Rules

Solo users tend to live in schedules and manual blocks. Recovery users often need harder overrides and stricter exits.

Visibility

Partner and family flows add approval states, alerts, linked devices, and cross-user accountability layers.

Tone

Customization depth matters. The same blocker should be able to feel supportive, firm, or highly corrective.
