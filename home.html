Permission and trust clarity

FocusForge explains what it needs and keeps that explanation plain.

What Accessibility is used for

Accessibility is used so FocusForge can detect which app is currently in the foreground and apply the blocking rules the user chose.

FocusForge is not using Accessibility to read typed content, messages, or general screen contents.

Usage Access

Usage Access is optional and powers app-time insights when enabled.

Cloud-backed features

Cloud-backed features may rely on configured providers such as Firebase and Google Play billing.

User control

Users can review policies, manage permissions in Android settings, and request deletion of cloud-backed account data.

FAQ

Clear answers to the questions people usually ask first.

What does FocusForge do?

FocusForge is an Android focus and enforcement app built around configurable app blocking, Lock In sessions, progression systems, accountability layers, and optional family controls.

Why does it need Accessibility?

Accessibility lets FocusForge detect which app is currently in the foreground so it can apply the blocking rules the user chose.

Does it read messages or typed text?

No. FocusForge does not use Accessibility to read typed content, messages, or general screen contents.

Can I manage or cancel subscriptions?

Subscriptions are handled through Google Play billing. Billing and refund details are linked in the policy pages.

Can I request account deletion?

Yes. FocusForge provides account deletion support and a dedicated delete-account page for cloud-backed account data requests.

Does it support accountability or family controls?

Yes. FocusForge includes optional partner and family layers for users who want shared accountability or managed household structure.
